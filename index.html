Post Mortem

In this project, I successfully completed a series of tasks. Using C++ and SDL3, I implemented an engine responsible for managing game objects and resources. This engine played a crucial role in game development, handling tasks such as graphics rendering, resource loading, and management. I also made the engine data-driven through pybind11, allowing scripts for game objects creating and scene data to be loaded at runtime. To enhance the game's appeal and challenge, I introduced hidden wall elements in the game design, offering players a fresh, strategic gaming experience (which can be my 'wow' factor). Additionally, I utilized SDL3 Mixer in C++ to implement an audio component, enriching the game with background music and various sound effects, making it more vibrant and immersive.

Despite achieving some success, I acknowledge certain shortcomings in the project. Due to time and technical constraints, I couldn't realize all ideas and functionalities. For instance, I could enhance user engagement by allowing them to adjust the map size for increased editing freedom, add enemy movement patterns to chase the player for heightened difficulty, display scores on-screen for improved user feedback, and incorporate animated effects like breaking walls or collecting jewels to enhance visual appeal and enjoyment.

Overall, I've learned a great deal from this project. After dedicating over 6 hours daily and undergoing significant code revisions, I'm satisfied with my current progress. Reflecting on the project's experiences and shortcomings will better prepare me for future endeavors, enabling me to continually elevate my technical skills and game development capabilities.
